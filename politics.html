THE HORMUZ MEDIATOR: PAKISTAN AT THE CENTER OF GLOBAL DIPLOMACY

By Geopolitical Desk | May 7, 2026

ISLAMABAD: As the global energy market shudders under the weight of the Strait of Hormuz blockade, Pakistan has emerged as the unlikely pivot point for world peace. Sources within the Foreign Office confirm that President Donald Trump is currently reviewing a 14-point "De-escalation Roadmap" transmitted via Islamabad.

The proposal, which seeks to secure safe passage for commercial vessels through the "Project Freedom" initiative, positions Pakistan as the sole credible intermediary between Washington and Tehran. With Iranian state media acknowledging the receipt of the US response, the "Islamabad Channel" has become the world’s most critical diplomatic artery. If successful, this could reset Pakistan's standing in the international community, transforming a domestic economic crisis into a global strategic triumph.

THE RS. 400 BARRIER: ECONOMIC PRESSURE TESTS POLITICAL STABILITY

By News Desk | May 7, 2026

KARACHI: The "Awaam" (people) are facing a historic squeeze as fuel prices hit an all-time high of Rs. 399.86 per liter. As the city runs on fumes, the government's latest hike—necessitated by the soaring cost of oil imports amid the Middle East conflict—has pushed the middle class to its breaking point.

Coming on the first anniversary of "Marka-e-Haq," the celebratory mood is tempered by a looming IMF board review. While PM Shehbaz Sharif sounds optimistic about a $1.2 billion tranche, the political cost of these reforms is mounting. In Quetta and Lahore, calls for nationwide strikes are growing, testing the "Stability First" hybrid model that has governed the federation since late 2024. The question remains: can the government balance its role as a global peacemaker while its own streets are fueled by economic frustration?
